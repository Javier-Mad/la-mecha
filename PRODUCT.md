# La Mecha

## Product Purpose

A Spanish-language push-your-luck intimacy card game for couples. Players take turns holding a "fuse" — each push draws a new card and shortens the fuse. Three BOOM triggers: draw a BOOM card, push to zero, or attempt a third consecutive push. The game ends when a VICTORIA wild card is drawn. Entirely client-side; no server, no accounts.

## Users

Two people, same room, same device. Evening or night context. Intimate and slightly tense atmosphere. Mobile held in hand — thumb reach is everything.

## Register

Product. The design serves the game session, not the brand.

## Core experience

The fuse is the central metaphor — it must feel physically tense. Screens are brief interruptions between turns. Every tap must feel tactile, immediate, satisfying. The ambient darkness and ember palette are the world of the game; UI chrome should dissolve into it.

## Anti-references

- Clinical SaaS dashboards with card grids
- Neon-on-black gaming aesthetics (too aggressive)
- Pastel wellness apps
- Heavy glassmorphism for its own sake

## Strategic principles

- Every animation serves spatial or state communication, not decoration
- Trust the player: no confirmation dialogs for game actions
- Dark, amber/ember palette is the tone — not a style choice
